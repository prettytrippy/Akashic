In the head of the adoring male is the illusion that sublime beauty “is all head and wings, with no bottom to betray” it.5 In one of Swift’s poems a young man explains the grotesque contradiction that is tearing him apart:6 Nor wonder how I lost my Wits; Oh! Caelia, Caelia, Caelia shits! In other words, in Swift’s mind there was an absolute contradiction “between the state of being in love and an awareness of the excremental function of the beloved.”7
