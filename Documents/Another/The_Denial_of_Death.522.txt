The psychoanalytic literature remained almost silent on the fear of death until the late 1930’s and World War II. And the reason was as Rank revealed: how could psychoanalytic therapy scientifically cure the terror of life and death? But it could cure the problems of sex, which it itself posited.18 But more to the point of our discussion is whether the fiction of the death instinct revealed anything in Freud’s personal attitude toward reality.
