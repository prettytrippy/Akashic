When Fliess predicted Freud’s death at 51, according to his calculations, Freud “thought it more likely he would die in the forties from rupture of the heart.”20 When the age of 51 passed uneventfully, “Freud adopted another superstitious belief—that he had to die in February, 1918.”21 Freud often wrote and spoke to his disciples about his growing old, that he had not long to live.
