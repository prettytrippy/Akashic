The upshot of all this is that personal heroism through individuation is a very daring venture precisely because it separates the person out of comfortable “beyonds.” It takes a strength and courage the average man doesn’t have and couldn’t even understand—as Jung so well points out.28 The most terrifying burden of the creature is to be isolated, which is what happens in individuation: one separates himself out of the herd.
