Clinically we have huge documentation for this character type whom we call the psychopath; phenomenologically we have understood this since Dewey’s Experience and Nature.14 Brown’s whole thesis falls then, on a twin failure: not only on his failure to understand the real psychodynamics of guilt, but also his turning his back on how the child registers experience on his body: the need to develop in a dualistic way in order to be a rich repository of life.15
