On the contrary, he lost himself, owing to the fact that this self was seen fantastically reflected in the possible.25 Of course, this description touches everyday man as well at the extreme of schizophrenia, and it is just the cogency of Kierkegaard’s analysis that the two can be placed on the same continuum: Instead of summoning back possibility into necessity, the man pursues the possibility—and at last cannot find his way back to himself.26
