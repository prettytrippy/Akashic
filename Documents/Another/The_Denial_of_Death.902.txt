Rank calls this very aptly the “self-willed over-valuation of self” whereby the neurotic tries to cheat nature.15 He won’t pay the price that nature wants of him: to age, fall ill or be injured, and die. Instead of living experience he ideates it; instead of arranging it in action he works it all out in his head.
