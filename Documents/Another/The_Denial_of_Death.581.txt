Yet all this speculation is beside the point, because we are talking about Freud’s own perceptions and problems. From this point of view the significant thing about the occasion of the first fainting is that the talk of mummies came up because of Jung’s confusion about the corpses. Freud’s anxieties on both occasions are thereby tied to the same subjects of Egypt and the effacing of the father.
