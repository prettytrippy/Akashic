Anxiety was now seen largely as a matter of the reaction to global helplessness, abandonment, fate: I therefore maintain that the fear of death is to be regarded as an analogue of the fear of castration, and that the situation to which the ego reacts is the state of being forsaken or deserted by the protecting superego—by the powers of destiny—which puts an end to security against every danger.10
