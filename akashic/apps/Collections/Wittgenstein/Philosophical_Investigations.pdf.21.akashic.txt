The word “signify” is perhaps most straightforwardly applied when the name is actually a mark on the object signiﬁed. Suppose that the tools A uses in building bear certain marks. When A shows his assistant such a mark, the assistant brings the tool that has that mark on it. In this way, and in more or less similar ways, a name signiﬁes a thing, and is given to a thing. a When philosophizing, it will often prove use- ful to say to ourselves: naming something is rather like attaching a name tag to a thing. 16.
