Now then, did the sentence “Now I can go on” in case (151) mean the same as “Now the formula has occurred to me”, or something dif- ferent? We may say that in these circumstances the one sentence has the same sense (comes to the same thing) as the other. But also that in general, these two sentences do not have the same sense. We do say, “Now I can go on, I mean I know the formula,” as we say “I can walk, I mean I have time”; but also “I can walk, I mean I am already strong enough”; or “I can walk, as far as the state of my leg is concerned”, that is, when we are contrasting this condition for walking with others. But here we must be on our guard against thinking that there is some totality of conditions corresponding to the nature of each case (for  example, for a person’s walking) so that, as it were, he could not but walk if they were all fulﬁlled. 184.
