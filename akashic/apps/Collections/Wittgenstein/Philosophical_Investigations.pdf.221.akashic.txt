When I continue the interrupted sentence and say that this was how I had been going to continue it, this is similar to elaborating a train of thought from brief notes. Then don’t I interpret the notes? Was only one continuation pos- sible in these circumstances? Of course not. But I didn’t choose between these interpretations. And now it is as if I knew quite certainly what the whole picture represented. As if I could read the darkness. |164| 636. These ‘details’ are not irrelevant in the sense in which other  circumstances, which I can also remember, are irrelevant. He needn’t know, for instance, that I had already opened my mouth to speak. But he can ‘ﬁll out the picture’ in this way.
