This ﬁnds expression in the question of the essence of language, of propositions, of thought. a For although we, in our investigations, are trying to understand the nature of language a its function, its struc- ture a yet this is not what that question has in view. For it sees the essence of things not as something that already lies open to view, and that becomes surveyable through a process of ordering, but as some- thing that lies beneath the surface. Something that lies within, which we perceive when we see right into the thing, and which an analysis is supposed to unearth. ‘The essence is hidden from us’: this is the form our problem now assumes. We ask: “What is language?”, “What is a proposition?” And the answer to these questions is to be given once for all, and indepen- dently of any future experience. 93.
