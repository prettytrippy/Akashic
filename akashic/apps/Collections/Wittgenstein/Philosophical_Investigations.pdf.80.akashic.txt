At bottom, giving “This is how things are” as the general form of propositions is the same as giving the explanation: a proposition is whatever can be true or false. For instead of “This is how things are”, I could just as well have said “Such-and-such is true”. (Or again, “Such- and-such is false”.) But ‘p’ is true = p ‘p’ is false = not-p. What engages with the concept of truth (as with a cog-wheel) is a  proposition. But this is a bad picture. It is as if one were to say “The chess king is the piece that one puts in check”.
