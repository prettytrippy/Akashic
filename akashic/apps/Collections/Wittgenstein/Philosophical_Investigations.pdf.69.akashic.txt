Why do we say that a proposition is something remarkable? On the one hand, because of the enormous importance attaching to it. (And that is correct.) On the other hand, this importance, together with a misunderstanding |44| of the logic of language, seduces us into think- ing that something extraordinary, even unique, must be achieved by propo- sitions. a A misunderstanding makes it look to us as if a proposition did something strange. 94. ‘Remarkable things, propositions!’ Here we already have the sub- limation of our whole account of logic. The tendency to assume a pure intermediary between the propositional sign and the facts.
