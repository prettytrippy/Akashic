Philosophy must not interfere in any way with the actual use of language, so it can in the end only describe it. For it cannot justify it either. It leaves everything as it is. It is not the business of philosophy to resolve a contradiction by means of a mathematical or logico-mathematical discovery, but to ren- der surveyable the state of mathematics that troubles us a the state of affairs before the contradiction is resolved. (And in doing this one is not sidestepping a difﬁculty.) Here the fundamental fact is that we lay down rules, a technique, for playing a game, and that then, when we follow the rules, things don’t turn out as we had assumed. So that we are, as it were, entan- gled in our own rules. This entanglement in our rules is what we want to understand: that is, to survey. It throws light on our concept of meaning something.
